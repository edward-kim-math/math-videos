Find an equation of the line passing through the points (-2,4) and (6,8).
There are many possible equations for the same line. All answers require finding slope. When you have slope and a point (and note, we actually have *two* points), then the point-slope form is fastest.
To compute the slope, we can do the y-value 8 minus the y-value 4 on top, and the bottom would then have to be the x-value 6 minus the x-value -2.
This simplifies to 4 over 8, which simplifies to 1 over 2.
We can also find the slope by doing the y-value 4 minus the y-value 8 on top, but then we have follow that order, and the bottom would then have to be the x-value -2 minus the x-value 6.
This simplifies to -4 over -8, which simplifies to 1 over 2. Either way, we get the same slope of one half.
One acceptable final answer is to use the point-slope form with the point (6, 8) and the slope one half. Then, we have y minus 8 equals one half times the quantity x minus 6.
Another acceptable final answer is to use the point-slope form with the point (-2, 4) and the slope one half. Then, we have y minus 4 equals one half times the quantity x plus 2. The reason we have x plus 2 is this simplifies the original x minus negative 2.
Say we needed an answer in slope-intercept form. We can start from either of our two equations above. If we start from y minus 8 equals one half times the quantity x minus 6, then we can distribute one half on the right, so the right side ends up being one half x minus 3.
Adding 8 to both sides, we get y equals one half x plus 5.
